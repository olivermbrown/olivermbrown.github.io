YouTube

I run a YouTube channel as a hobby, on which I have produced videos explaining many interesting topics in mathematics and physics. The length of these videos varies but typically last between thirty minutes and an hour. As such, they often comprise short lecture series on particular topics, and make for a good introduction to research level topics. They are intended to be accessible to anyone with a basic understanding of calculus and linear algebra.

You can find my channel here.

Topics that I have covered include:

  • The Casimir effect
  • Dynamical systems, bifurcation theory and critical slowing down
  • Topology in quantum mechanics
  • Quantum computing
  • Particle physics
